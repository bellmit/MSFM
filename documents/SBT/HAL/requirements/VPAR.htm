Virtual Par Requirements

 

Miscellaneous items

 

1)       If at anytime after an order is received from TPF, and before it is filled or routed back, the trade server “crashes”, upon failover the order will be booked and will participate in the open.  This includes any order routed back from PAR that should get the firm quote obligation, but it will not.

 

2)       The firm quote obligation can be ignored for any order routed to PAR that is routed back to CBOEDirect after a failover.

 

3)       Before booking any order (not including quote type orders), always check the current NBBO and see if it should be routed to PAR.

 

4)       No VPAR related behavior shall be visible at initial rollout.  VPAR should be able to be turned on with a single XML setting.

 

5)       RFP responses will not be part of the disseminated quote.

 

6)       The firm quote obligation does not apply to Tweeners, only to Tweener Locks and NBBO rejects.

 

7)       The users will see the RFP as an auction.

 

 

Accept order

 

1)       The system receives a new order from TPF.

 

2)       The system determines the NBBO at the time the system begins processing the order.

 

3)       If the order from TPF…

(the first condition to apply is the one that is used)

 

a) is routed back for the firm quote obligation; go to sub-flow Firm Quote Trade.  This flow ends.

                                                                     i)      TPF will indicate that the order could not be sent away.

 

b) is not marketable against CBOE or the NBBO and does not improve the CBOE market (i.e. not a tweener); then the order is booked.  This flow ends.

 

c) is marketable against an order currently being flashed; then the “flash” ends and the orders are traded.

a)       If the flashed order has remaining quantity, it is routed to PAR for auto-linking

b)       If the incoming order has remaining quantity, the system gets the current NBBO and continues to (4d).

 

d) is processed when CBOE is at or better than the NBBO; then the order will be handled as before.  This flow ends.

 

e) is an NBBO reject, Tweener or Tweener Lock and a quote trigger, auction, or quote lock exists; then the current trigger, auction or quote lock is ended and the conditions listed under this requirement (3) are reevaluated.

 

f) is an NBBO reject, Tweener or Tweener lock.  Follow the processing starting at requirement (4).

 

 

4)       An RFP will be sent to all users with “quotes” in the class specified by the order.  This begins the “flash” phase.

a)       The price of the RFP will be the current NBBO price on the opposite side of the order.

 

5)       For each RFP sent the system shall record which market makers are quoting at the best price, and for each, the size of their quote at that price and this flow ends.

·         Firm Quote obligation does not apply to tweeners

 

 

Accept Quote or RFP

Precondition: A VPAR auction exists.  If not, no VPAR processing is necessary.

 

6)       An RFP that is received that is not marketable against the VPAR order is rejected.

 

7)       If a VPAR auction is in progress (flash or trigger) and the quote is on the same side as the flashed order at a better price, the VPAR auction is ended and the order is routed to PAR.

 

8)       If CBOE is the NBBO on the side opposite the VPAR order because the away market’s quote changed

a)       End the current auction.

b)       trade the VPAR order’s remaining quantity (if any) against the current market up to and including the new NBBO price. 

c)       If there is quantity remaining, create a new VPAR auction following the steps described in Accept Order.

 

9)       If the away market(s) improved their quote(s) on the side opposite the VPAR order, end the current auction and route the order to PAR (if there is quantity remaining).

 

10)   If a VPAR auction is “flashing” and the quote/RFP is marketable against the VPAR order, a trigger is created.

 

11)   If a VPAR auction is in trigger and the quote/RFP is marketable against the VPAR order, the quote/RFP joins the trigger.

 

 

 

Firm Quote Trade

 

12)   An order is received back from TPF that CBOEDirect sent to PAR.  TPF indicates that this order could not be routed away.

 

13)   The system looks up the best price and quantity the order would have received prior to routing away.

 

14)   The system attempts to trade up to and including this price.

·         This trade could cause an NBBO trade through if the NBBO moved en route from PAR to CBOEDirect.

 

15)   If the quantity traded is less than that which would have been traded originally, the order is due a fill from the MMs originally quoting, otherwise continue at requirement (17).

 

16)   Prior to filling the order, the MM’s firm quote obligations are reduced by the amount that has just traded (if any).

a)                   First subtract from each MM’s obligation any amount that they just traded

b)                   For the remainder traded (by other MMs, customers, etc.), reduce each MM’s obligation by distributing it using UMA.

                                                                     i)      The quote quantities to use in the UMA calculation should be the net obligation amount after 16a.

 

17)   Allocate each MM up to their remaining firm quote obligation using CUMA.

a)       Do not reduce their current quote

b)       Indicate on the fill report this fill was due to their firm quote obligation

 

18)   Handle any remaining quantity by starting processing at Accept Order; however now treat this order as a new order, not a firm quote obligation.
